Sextant exclusion rule
If only 1 tooth remains in a sextant, it is included in the adjacent sextant. If no teeth remain, the sextant is marked X.

Step 3 — Confirm periodontitis case definition (Tonetti et al. 2018)
A periodontitis case requires one of the following:
Criterion A
Interdental CAL detectable at ≥2 non-adjacent teeth, not attributable to non-periodontal causes
Criterion B
Buccal or oral CAL ≥3 mm with pocketing >3 mm at ≥2 teeth, not attributable to non-periodontal causes
CAL must not be attributable to: traumatic tooth brushing, gingival recession, cervical caries, restorations, endodontic lesions

Key clinical reminders (Dr Ball, DOH256)
Classification purpose
Frameworks for aetiology, pathogenesis, treatment; enable research comparability
Gingivitis reversibility
Dental plaque-induced gingivitis is fully reversible with adequate plaque control
Aggressive periodontitis removed
The 2017 classification abolished the aggressive/chronic distinction; grading captures disease velocity instead
Stage vs grade
Stage = severity & complexity at a point in time. Grade = rate of progression & risk profile
Default assumption
Clinicians should initially assume Grade B and seek evidence to upgrade to C or downgrade to A
Gingivitis sub-classification
BOP is the only objective measure for gingivitis. Uses full-mouth BoP% thresholds (<10%, 10–30%, >30%)
